Welcome

Welcome to "A Beginner's Guide to Game of Thrones"!


This guide will catch you up to the events happening at the beginning of season 1, so it isn't too difficult to get to know the characters while trying to figure out why some characters feel the way they do about other characters, and who belongs to which house.


Keep in mind that this is simply to help better understand character motivations and the bigger points of history, so there will be a lot of information left out. With how information dense A Song of Ice and Fire (the series Game of Thrones is based on) is, an entire library could be written. This is just to start you out and get your feet wet. Some details (such as character's ages and timelines) vary slightly from books to T.V series. This guide is according to the T.V series timelines.


Enjoy!

White Walkers

Several thousand years before the events of the show, a winter known as the Long Night descended upon the world, which lasted an entire generation. Thousands starved as the crops and fields lay buried under dozens of feet of snow. In the darkness and cold of the Long Night, the White Walkers descended upon Westeros from the farthest north.


White Walkers have the ability to reanimate corpses to kill the living at their command (these corpses are called "wights".) The conflict that arose from the Long Night is known as the "War for the Dawn" as the White Walkers sought to bring an end to all life and to cover the world in an endless winter.


Facing annihilation, the First Men rallied together with the Children of the Forest (don't worry about who they are for now) and drove the White Walkers back into the uttermost north.


In the present day, most believe that the Long Night is just a children's story, and that the White Walkers are nothing more than legend. Even the few who believe they did once exist think they went extinct thousands of years ago.

The Wall

After defeating the White Walkers, The Wall, a massive fortification standing seven hundred feet high and stretching from one side of the continent to the other, was constructed along the northernmost isthmus of northern Westeros to bar the Walkers' return.


Legend says that the Wall was infused with powerful magic spells by the Children of the Forest that prevent the White Walkers from crossing it. The ancient order of the Night's watch was founded to defend the Wall should the White Walkers return to invade the realms of men once more.


While originally serving in the Night's Watch was considered an honor, now the Wall is extremely undermanned - typically by convicts and criminals who have been pardoned on the condition that they live out their days as a member of the Night's Watch.

House Targaryen

House Targaryen - originally from Valyria on the continent of Essos to the east - was the royal, ruling house over the continent of Westeros (comprised of 7 kingdoms) for the past few hundred years. They are known for their platinum blonde hair, and the fact that they historically rode on the backs of dragons.

Aerys "The Mad King" Targaryen: The Mad King was the last member of the Targaryen house to rule Westeros. Although his reign began benevolently, he eventually became mad and burned Ned Stark's father and brother alive in the throne room. He also planted wildfire (an explosive liquid) under the entire city of Kings Landing and threatened to "burn them all". He was killed by the captain of his Kingsguard, Jaime Lannister.





Rhaegar Targaryen: Rhaegar was the eldest son of the Mad King. Although married with children, about 17 years before the events of the first episode Rhaegar fell in love with Lyanna Stark and stole her away from Winterfell (her home, and the seat of House Stark). It was this act that started a revolution called "Robert's Rebellion", where Robert Baratheon (Lyanna's betrothed), Ned Stark (Lyanna's brother), and many others rose up to overthrow the Mad King and place Robert in his stead. Rhaegar was killed by Robert in the Battle at the Trident.






Viserys Targaryen: Viserys was a young boy at the time of Robert's Rebellion. When the Mad King was overthrown, he, his mother, and his unborn sister Daenerys, fled from Kings Landing and eventually ended up in Essos (another continent across the Narrow Sea from Westeros). His mother died in childbirth, and he and Daenerys have been fleeing Robert's assassins up until the point that the show begins.






Daenerys Targaryen: Daenerys is the youngest daughter of the Mad King. She and her brother Viserys are the last Targaryens known to be alive. She has been fleeing Robert's assassins with her brother since before she was born.




House Baratheon

Robert Baratheon: Robert was the first from House Baratheon to rule Westeros after overthrowing the Mad King. He married Cersei Lannister because it was a wise political move, not because he cared for her. He has never gotten over his love for Lyanna Stark, and has dealt with his grief through getting drunk and spending time with whores. He and Ned Stark were best friends growing up, but haven't seen each other in years when the series begins.




Cersei Lannister: Cersei is married to King Robert but she has an incestuous relationship with her twin brother Jaime. She has three children: Joffrey, Tommen, and Myrcella. Tommen and Myrcella are not pictured here, because they really don't have any role in the series until much later. Just know that they exist for now.




Joffrey Baratheon: Joffrey is first in line to take the throne when Robert dies. Not much of a spoiler to say that he is a prick. You find that out pretty quickly.







Stannis Baratheon: Stannis isn't shown on screen until Season 2, but for now just know that he is the Robert's younger brother and Renly's older brother. He doesn't have much of a relationship with either brother, and lives on Dragonstone (an island off the coast of Westeros).






Renly Baratheon: The youngest of the Baratheon brothers, Renly serves on Robert's small council.




House Lannister

House Lannister is well known for being extremely wealthy. It is also a common saying to hear, "A Lannister always pays his debts" which has come to have a double meaning: they will always pay back money that is due, and they will always pay back revenge that is due.

Tywin Lannister: Tywin is the patriarch of House Lannister, and the Lord of Casterly Rock (the seat of House Lannister). Tywin is cunning and ruthless. He served as Hand of the King to the Mad King, but ended up helping to overthrow him. He is one of, if not the, richest man in Westeros.





Cersei Lannister: Yes, yes, I know I already put Cersei under House Baratheon. But she is Baratheon simply on technicality. Her picture belongs under house Lannister, because that is who she truly is.





Jaime Lannister: Jaime is most well known for killing the Mad King while serving in the Kingsguard. He has since been given the nickname "Kingslayer". Typically he is only called that in a derogatory manner, referring to his betrayel and murder of someone he was sworn to protect. He is also well known as one of the best sword fighters alive. He is now serving as Kingsguard to Robert Baratheon, and has an incestuous relationship with his twin sister, Cersei.




Tyrion Lannister: Sometimes called "the Imp", Tyrion is the youngest of Tywin's children. His mother died in childbirth giving birth to him, and for that reason (as well as him being a dwarf) he is hated by his father and his sister. He has a closer relationship with his brother Jaime, however. Tyrion is well known for being witty and clever.






Lancel Lannister: Lancel is the son of Kevan (Tywin's brother - not pictured, because he doesn't have a large role to play) so he is cousin to Cersei, Jaime, and Tyrion. He is currently serving as King Robert's squire.


House Stark

Eddard "Ned" Stark: Ned Stark is the Warden of the North and the head of House Stark. He wasn't supposed to have these titles, but when his older brother and father were killed by the Mad King, the mantle was thrust upon him. He is known for being a very honorable and trustworthy man. The only thing to argue against that is when he came home from war after Robert's Rebellion, he came home with another son, Jon Snow.







Catelyn Stark: Catelyn is fiercly loyal, which may be why she was so hurt and betrayed when Ned came home with another son. For this reason, she still to this day hates Jon Snow as he is a constant reminder of her husband's infidelity.





Robb Stark: Ned and Catelyn's oldest son, and first in line to become Lord of Winterfell.





Jon Snow: Jon Snow is Ned Stark's bastard son. Snow is the last name given to bastards in the north (each of the 7 kingdoms has their own last name given to bastards). Not much is known about Jon's mother at this point, other than Ned tells Robert that her name was Wylla and that she was a whore he met during Robert's Rebellion. Catelyn Stark hates Jon, and Sansa is cruel to him as well, but he has a close relationship with the rest of the Stark household.


Sansa Stark: Ned and Catelyn's oldest girl, Sansa is drawn to fancy things and enjoys feeling like a lady.


Arya Stark: Arya is the youngest girl in the Stark household, and is the exact opposite of Sansa. She is a tomboy, and prefers swordfighting to sewing.


Bran Stark: One of the younger Stark boys.


Rickon Stark: You could be forgiven for not knowing that Rickon exists until a few seasons into the show. He is shown on screen in the first season, but doesn't have much dialogue, if any. Just know that he exists for now, and that he is the youngest Stark.


Theon Greyjoy: 8 years before the events in the series, the Greyjoys in the Iron Islands rebelled against the Iron Throne and sought to claim their independence. Their self proclaimed king, Balon Greyjoy, is Theon's father. After the rebellion was crushed, Balon Greyjoy was accepted back into the King's peace, keeping his lordship and titles, but only on the condition that his last surviving son - Theon - be made a ward of House Stark as hostage for Balon's good behavior. However, Theon has become more of a member of the family, rather than a prisoner, in the last several years living with the Starks.


Lyanna Stark: Lyanna is Ned Stark's younger sister, and was betrothed to marry Robert Baratheon. However, Rhaegar Targaryen stole her away from Winterfell and it isn't known at this point what happened to her afterward. It was her being taken away from Winterfell, combined with the burning of her brother and father on orders from the Mad King that started Robert's Rebellion and the eventual replacement of the Mad King by Robert Baratheon. Although we don't know what exactly happened to Lyanna at this point, we know that she never returned to Winterfell alive.
